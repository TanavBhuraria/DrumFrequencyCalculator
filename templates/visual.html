How it works
Modes: Standing oscillates in place (nodes fixed). Traveling shifts sideways. Hybrid multiplies a spatial phase shift (drift) with a time oscillation so the pattern both oscillates and slowly drifts.

Harmonics: adding integer multiples of the base spatial frequency creates higher modes (like drum overtones). Use the harmonics controls to set relative amplitudes.

Energy: local energy shown by color is computed from approximate kinetic term \((\partial_t y)^2\) and potential term \((\partial_x y)^2\). Brighter = more local motion/strain.

This panel is educational only; the visualiser uses clamping and adaptive sampling to avoid aliasing and breakage at extreme values.
Defaults are safe. Try extremes; the visualiser will clamp and adapt sampling automatically.
